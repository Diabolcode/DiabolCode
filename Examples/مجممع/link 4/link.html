1)What is SEO?

SEO is an acronym for Search Engine Optimization, or "earch Engine Optimization" and expresses the practices that aim to increase and raise the quality of web traffic (the number of visits to a particular website), through unpaid search results on search engines, what is known as "Organic Search Results" means the regular unpaid search. Although the term is an acronym for "search engine optimization" , this concept is more about people than search engines. It is about:

  • understanding what users are searching for on the web.
  • The answers they seek to find.
  • The words they use in their research.
  • The type of content they consume.

2)Is seo important?

Although paid ads and social media platforms are able to attract more visitors to your website, it is just that organic search results appear to professional searchers more reliable and get more clicks than paid ads. As of the search results shown to users in theUnited States of America, users click on only 2.8% of paid results. Not only that, as SEO is the only online marketing method that, if you use it well, will generate continuous profits for you.


3)What is the purpose behind search engines?

Optimizing your site for search engines may seem to you a difficult task, but you must know that search engines actually want you to succeed, and they will help you with that too.


4)What is the most important step you will do before working on the code?

It is important to know the intent of the user, but before doing that, you need to first define the main objective of your website so that you can come up with a proper SEO plan.


Information on the most common user intentions:

  • Where the user is looking for a certain piece of information, for example: "best scholarships abroad".
  • Browse: the user searches for a specific site, such as: www.Daibolcode.com
  • Transactional: In which the user seeks to perform a particular transaction such as a purchase or sale. For example: "Computers for sale at attractive prices."
